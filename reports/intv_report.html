TL;DR

Interview Looms are an excellent source — far better than group meetings. Each is a 1:1 call where the customer talks to camera in a large tile, so the crop-the-tile + keep-audio recipe trains cleanly every time. All four twinned + cloned + rendered.

Why interviews work where group meetings don't: a 1:1 interview puts the customer in a large, cameras-on tile and they talk for most of the call — exactly what the trainer needs. Group war-rooms are screen-share with tiny presenter tiles. Look identity tracks face clarity: unobstructed faces (Crystal 0.82, Jen 0.84) score high; a cap + glasses (Marc) depresses ArcFace even though the looks visibly match.

5Method

Per interview: pull the Loom recording (yt-dlp --cookies-from-browser) → identify the customer's tile (vs the HeyGen interviewer) → crop head-and-shoulders, upscale, keep the audio track → Avatar V twin via /v2/video_avatar + bundled R&D consent → clone voice from a solo-speaking span → 2 Image-N looks (ArcFace-scored) → 4-beat emotion arc + generated-look demo (ElevenLabs v3) → host. Identity = ArcFace cosine vs a source frame.

Companion to the public Existing Footage → Avatar hub (YouTube/IG/TikTok/Meet). This interviews edition is internal/org-only.
